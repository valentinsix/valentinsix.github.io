- In academia, it can feel like people work all the time (even on weekends, late at night...). Most people are inded pasionate about their topic of research, but surely it can't be that healthy to just do the same thing all the time. 
- I tseems like in the world of research, a typical "company work schedule" is almost never adopted. 
- In research, especially during a PhD, wheer you pretty much on your own, the amount of progress that you achieve depends on you alone. Therefore more work hours = more progress ?
- I believe that it would be useful to have a clear separation between your private life and your research work. First, you cant bevery productive, all the time. Second, you need to take care of yourself (and maybe others). Third, it's better to work with full focus during a reasonable amount of time than to work all the time with low intensity.
