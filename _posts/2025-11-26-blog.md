I thought about starting a blog a few years ago, but I never found a convincing reason to do it.
I don’t have a brand to promote, no audience to grow, and I don’t particularly enjoy writing just for the sake of it.

So I decided to approach blogging differently. This is, first and foremost, a space for me.

I won’t spend hours polishing every post. I’ll write to clear my thoughts and collect ideas in one place. I could keep everything private, but I might as well share it, as someone might stumble across something useful.

It will probably be a mess at first. Ideas will overlap, and occasionally go nowhere. Over time, I hope to revisit old thoughts to cluster things together (and pretend there was the plan all along).

I call this blog **Latent Thoughts**. Most of what ends up here starts as half-formed ideas that I don’t want to lose. I often write something down, close the tab, and come back later when it finally makes sense. Or when I realize it didn’t. In that sense, these posts are similar to checkpoints during training.

The main purposes of this blog are:

**Articulate clear ideas**:
Thinking is messy. It’s easy to lose good ideas or to rediscover the same ones multiple times and feel briefly proud each time. Writing things down (especially on paper in my case) helps me develop complex ideas without constantly starting from scratch. Writing forces thoughts to take shape. I see writing and thinking as two sides of the same process: to write is to think clearly enough that your ideas survive the structure of language.

**Centralize ideas in one place**:
I tend to have the same ideas repeatedly, mostly because I forget I already had them. This blog is my attempt at a small garden for those thoughts. This blog is a way to plant those thoughts in a small garden: hopefully I keep track of what's growing, and I revisit it now and then.
